Calculus in Agro Product Technology

Applying Derivatives to Harumanis Mango Growth Kinetics (Malaysia)

The Bioscience Scenario

In Agro Product Technology, predicting harvest yields and optimizing inputs (like NPK fertilizers) requires understanding plant growth kinetics. The Harumanis mango (MA 128) undergoes distinct growth phases over its ~12-week development cycle on the tree.

Instead of just looking at the total weight, agronomists calculate the rate of weight gain (the derivative). Applying fertilizer precisely when the growth rate is at its highest (peak derivative) ensures maximum nutrient absorption, leading to larger, sweeter fruits.

Mathematical Model

*Data simulated based on empirical growth parameters from Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute (MARDI) horticulture publications (e.g., mardi.gov.my/horticulture-data).

1. Total Fruit Weight Function $W(t)$:

Models the total mass of the mango (in grams) over time $t$ (in weeks).

$$ W(t) = -0.5t^3 + 8t^2 + 12t + 50 $$

2. Instantaneous Growth Rate $W'(t)$:

The first derivative calculates the exact rate of weight gain (grams per week) at any given moment.

$$ W'(t) = \frac{dW}{dt} = -1.5t^2 + 16t + 12 $$
